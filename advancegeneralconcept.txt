In Kafka, replication is crucial for fault tolerance and is managed at the Partition level:

Replica: Every partition has one or more replicas stored across different Kafka Brokers.

Replication Factor: This is the total number of copies (replicas) of a partition. A replication factor of 3 means there is the original partition plus two copies, stored on three different brokers.

Leader Replica: One replica is designated as the Leader. The Leader handles all producer write requests and consumer read requests for that partition.

Follower Replicas (In-Sync Replicas - ISRs): The other replicas are Followers. They constantly pull data from the Leader to keep their logs synchronized. If the Leader fails, one of the In-Sync Followers is automatically elected as the new Leader.

What are Composition and Aggregation? State the difference.
Composition and Aggregation are both forms of "has-a" relationships in object-oriented programming. They describe how one class contains another.

Composition is a strong relationship. The contained object cannot exist on its own and its lifecycle is dependent on the container. Think of a car and its engine. The engine can't exist outside of a car (in this context). When the car is destroyed, the engine is also gone.

Aggregation is a weak relationship. The contained object can exist independently of the container. A great example is a university and its professors. If the university closes, the professors still exist as individuals.
